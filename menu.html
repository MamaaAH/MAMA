neapolitan pizza

Our true artisanal Neapolitan pizzas respect all the traditions and techniques that have made it world-famous. They are handcrafted by using quality local and imported Italian ingredients, in a real AVPN-approved handmade Neapolitan oven!

Marinara

the sailor's pie


This is how it all started. Tomatoes were abundant, bread was cheap. Bakers put the two together. Sailors made it their go-to meal for long voyages, hence the name (marinaro=sailor in Italian). The original Neapolitan street food.

Components: Italian Tomatoes, Oregano, Garlic, Extra Virgin Olive Oil

125 LE

Cosacca

the king’s pie


This simple pizza was created in honour of the Russian King Nicholas II during his visit to Napoli.

Components: Italian Tomatoes, Garlic, Oregano, Parmigiano Reggiano DOP, Fresh Basil, Extra Virgin Olive Oil, Black Pepper

175 LE

Scarpariello

the shoemaker’s pie


It is said that shoemakers invented this recipe (scarparo = shoemaker in the Neapolitan dialect) out of a desire to eat something quick, tasty and nutritious while working. Also traditionally a pasta dish.

Components: Italian Tomatoes, Garlic, Italian Sun-dried Tomatoes, Oregano, Parmigiano Reggiano DOP, Fresh Basil, Extra Virgin Olive Oil, Black Pepper

195 LE

Diavola

the devil’s pie


TBefore the “pepperoni” pizzas you know, Neapolitans put spicy salami on a Margherita base. As the name suggests, this fiery combo is perfect for those looking for a flavourful kick to their pizza.

Components: Italian Tomatoes, Fresh Mozzarella, Salami, Chilli flakes, Fresh Basil, Parmigiano Reggiano DOP, Extra Virgin Olive Oil

270 LE

Romana

the roman pie


In Rome, it’s called a Neapolitan. But we make Neapolitan pizza, so for us, this anchovy-topped Margherita is Roman.

Components: Italian Tomatoes, Fresh Mozzarella, Anchovies, Fresh Basil, Parmigiano Reggiano DOP, Extra Virgin Olive Oil

285 LE

the wild one
